What To Include
  • The issue you can already see: denials, payer drag, provider onboarding, workflow breakdowns, documentation burden, or reporting gaps.
  • Which part of the practice is feeling it most: front desk, providers, billing, credentialing, operations, or leadership.
  • Any timing pressure tied to growth, hiring, payer issues, or current-system frustration.
What Not To Send

No patient PHI in the first message. A high-level business description is the right starting point.

What Happens Next

The first response should help you decide on a clean next step.

The goal is not to create more back-and-forth. The goal is to confirm fit, identify the likely area of friction, and point you toward the most useful next move.

01

We review the issue

Your note gives us the operating context around the problem you already see.

02

We identify fit

We determine whether the issue is best addressed as billing, credentialing, workflow, systems, documentation, or broader operations support.

03

You get direction

That may mean a consultation, a workflow review, or a more focused starting point linked to one service path.

04

No need to over-prepare

A concise description of the business issue is enough to begin.

FAQ

Common questions before reaching out.

Do I need to know exactly which service I need before contacting you?

No. If the issue is still mixed together, describe the operating problem and we can help point you to the right starting place.

Can I reach out if the problem touches more than one part of the practice?

Yes. That is often the reason teams reach out in the first place.

Should I include patient examples or screenshots?

No patient PHI in initial contact. Keep the first message at the workflow and business level.
